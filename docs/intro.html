🌊 什麼是灰色地帶活動？

「灰色地帶」是指介於和平與戰爭之間的模糊狀態。在台灣周邊海域，這包括：大量漁船越界作業、船隻關閉 AIS 定位訊號成為「暗船」、海上民兵偽裝為漁船活動，以及與軍事演習高度相關的異常船舶行為。

這些活動不構成傳統意義上的軍事衝突，卻持續侵蝕海上秩序、消耗監測資源，並可能為進一步行動創造條件。透過衛星與 AIS 數據的交叉比對，我們能追蹤這些隱蔽的海上活動。

🌊 What is Gray Zone Activity?

The "gray zone" refers to the ambiguous space between peace and war. Around Taiwan, this includes: fishing fleets crossing maritime boundaries, vessels disabling AIS transponders to become "dark ships," maritime militia disguised as fishing boats, and anomalous vessel behavior closely correlated with military exercises.

These activities fall short of traditional military conflict but continuously erode maritime order, drain monitoring resources, and may create conditions for further action. By cross-referencing satellite imagery and AIS data, we can track these covert maritime activities.

🔌 海底電纜與灰色地帶

台灣超過 95% 的對外網路通訊依賴海底電纜。這些電纜集中通過台灣海峽與周邊海域——正是灰色地帶活動最頻繁的區域。

拖網漁船的錨泊與作業是電纜損壞的主因之一。當大量不明船隻在電纜路線附近活動，便構成潛在威脅。本站地圖圖層可疊加顯示海底電纜路線與船隻分布，協助識別風險。

🔌 Submarine Cables & the Gray Zone

Over 95% of Taiwan's international internet traffic relies on submarine cables. These cables pass through the Taiwan Strait and surrounding waters — precisely where gray zone activity is most intense.

Anchor dragging by fishing trawlers is a leading cause of cable damage. When unidentified vessels operate near cable routes, they pose a potential threat. Our map layers overlay submarine cable routes with vessel positions to help identify risks.

❓ 常見問題 FAQ

什麼是「暗船」(dark vessel)？

暗船是指關閉 AIS（船舶自動識別系統）訊號、無法被一般船舶追蹤系統偵測的船隻。本站透過 SAR 合成孔徑雷達衛星影像偵測這些未發送 AIS 訊號的船隻，並與 AIS 數據交叉比對，找出刻意「走暗」的可疑目標。

資料從哪裡來？

船舶位置資料來自台灣港務局公開的 AIS 數據；暗船偵測來自 Global Fishing Watch 的 SAR 衛星影像；威脅評分方法則參考美國智庫 CSIS「Signals in the Swarm」報告的灰色地帶分析方法論。所有資料皆為開源公開資訊（OSINT）。

資料多久更新一次？

AIS 船舶資料每 2 小時自動更新一次；完整資料管線（含暗船偵測、威脅評分、軌跡分析）每 12 小時執行一次；統計報告每週彙整。所有時間以 UTC（協調世界時）標示。

如何判定一艘船「可疑」？

本站採用 8 項指標的加權評分系統，包括：靠近海底電纜並低速徘徊、之字形異常航行、頻繁變更船名或呼號（身分操弄）、長時間關閉 AIS（走暗）、AIS 位置造假、海上旁靠（STS）等。分數達到門檻的船隻會被標記為可疑並列入清單。

海底電纜為什麼重要？

台灣超過 95% 的對外網路通訊依賴海底電纜，這些電纜密集通過台灣海峽。拖網漁船的錨泊與作業是電纜損壞的主因之一；當不明船隻在電纜路線附近長時間徘徊，便構成基礎設施安全威脅。

這是官方政府網站嗎？

不是。本站是獨立的開源情報（OSINT）專案，所有資料來自公開來源，分析方法與程式碼皆公開於 GitHub，不代表任何政府或官方機構立場。

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is a "dark vessel"?

A dark vessel is a ship that has switched off its AIS (Automatic Identification System) transponder so it cannot be tracked by ordinary vessel-tracking systems. This site detects them using SAR (synthetic aperture radar) satellite imagery and cross-references the detections against AIS data to surface targets that have deliberately "gone dark."

Where does the data come from?

Vessel positions come from the Taiwan Port Bureau's public AIS feed; dark-vessel detections come from Global Fishing Watch SAR satellite imagery; and the threat-scoring methodology is based on the CSIS "Signals in the Swarm" report on gray-zone analysis. All sources are open-source intelligence (OSINT).

How often is the data updated?

AIS vessel data refreshes every 2 hours; the full pipeline (dark-vessel detection, threat scoring, track analysis) runs every 12 hours; and statistical reports are compiled weekly. All timestamps are in UTC.

How is a vessel flagged as suspicious?

The site uses a weighted 8-criterion scoring system: loitering at low speed near submarine cables, zigzag navigation, frequent name/call-sign changes (identity manipulation), long AIS gaps (going dark), AIS position spoofing, ship-to-ship (STS) transfers, and more. Vessels that cross the score threshold are flagged and listed.

Why do submarine cables matter?

Over 95% of Taiwan's international internet traffic relies on submarine cables that pass densely through the Taiwan Strait. Anchor dragging by trawlers is a leading cause of cable damage, so unidentified vessels loitering near cable routes pose an infrastructure-security threat.

Is this an official government website?

No. This is an independent open-source intelligence (OSINT) project. All data comes from public sources, and the analysis methods and code are published on GitHub. It does not represent any government or official body.
